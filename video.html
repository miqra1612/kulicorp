Sungai Piring Airport is an airport serving Saraminda, the provincial capital city of South East Kalimantan, Indonesia. It is in the village of Sungai Piring at the North Saraminda district, close to the border with Muara Gajah. The airport started commercial operation on 12 April 2000. In 2008, Sungai Piring Airport handled 1.1 million passengers and already reach the maximum apron capacity, limiting airlines from operating into the airport.


In June 2008, the airport authority unveiled plans to develop the airport. The main focus is to improve the overall capacity and aircraft handling ability of the airport. Apron facilities will be upgraded, so they can handle 22 Boeing 737-800 aircraft from originally 8 Boeing 737-800. The project started in February 2010 and completed in September 2012.
